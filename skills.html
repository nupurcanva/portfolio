User Experience Design

Transforming Enterprise Collaboration Through Seamless Integration Design

When Microsoft Teams users needed to create visual content, they faced a fragmented experience that required constant context-switching between applications. I led the complete redesign of how Canva integrates within Microsoft Teams, taking ownership of the entire user journey from discovery to daily use.

I crafted an intuitive experience that allows users to create, edit, and share designs without leaving their Teams environment. This included designing smart notification systems that keep team members informed without overwhelming them, creating contextual onboarding that appears exactly when users need guidance, and developing error states that actually help users resolve issues rather than leaving them stuck. The visual language I developed maintains consistency with both platforms while feeling native to each. The result was a 40% reduction in support tickets and adoption by major enterprises including Deloitte.

Democratizing App Development Through Conversational Design

Traditional app development requires coding knowledge, creating a barrier for creative professionals who have great ideas but lack technical skills. I co-led the design of an innovative platform that allows anyone to build functional applications using natural language.

Instead of writing code, users simply describe what they want to create, and the system generates a working prototype. I designed the conversational interface to guide users through the development process, providing intelligent suggestions and catching potential issues before they become problems. The system I designed can understand intent from simple descriptions like "I need an app that helps users choose color palettes" and transform that into a functional tool. This breakthrough interface reduced app creation time from days to minutes and opened development to an entirely new audience of creators.

Experience Strategy

Positioning Design as the Native Language of AI Assistants

As AI assistants like ChatGPT and Claude became mainstream, a critical opportunity emerged: making visual design as natural in AI conversations as text. I led the strategic initiative to establish Canva as the default design platform across all major AI assistants.

This meant architecting a comprehensive framework for how AI assistants would understand and execute design tasks. I defined patterns for everything from simple requests ("make this more professional") to complex workflows ("create a brand-consistent social media campaign for product launch"). My strategy included designing for different user contexts—from free users discovering capabilities to enterprise teams needing branded outputs at scale.

I created systematic documentation that became the playbook for all AI integrations, ensuring consistency while allowing for each assistant's unique capabilities. This strategic foundation positioned Canva to capture the emerging market of AI-driven design, with early metrics showing 3x higher engagement when design tasks are available natively in AI conversations.

Reimagining Developer Engagement Through Human-Centered Personas

The developer ecosystem is often approached through a technical lens, missing the human motivations that drive platform adoption. I transformed our understanding of developers by leading comprehensive research that revealed distinct personas with unique needs, motivations, and barriers.

Through in-depth interviews and behavioral analysis, I identified key archetypes: the Entrepreneur building their first SaaS product, the Creative Developer bridging design and code, the Enterprise Architect ensuring scalability, and the Hobbyist exploring possibilities. Each persona came with specific journey maps, pain points, and success metrics.

I translated these insights into a living resource that product teams now use to validate every feature decision. This strategic shift from feature-first to persona-first development has resulted in more intuitive APIs, better documentation, and a 45% reduction in developer onboarding time.

User Research

Uncovering Hidden Enterprise Needs Through Embedded Research

Enterprise software often fails because it's designed for IT buyers rather than daily users. I pioneered an embedded research approach where I spent extended time with enterprise teams, observing their actual workflows rather than their reported needs.

Working with Fortune 500 companies, I discovered that the biggest barriers weren't technical but organizational—teams needed ways to maintain brand consistency across departments, manage permissions without IT bottlenecks, and track design usage for compliance. My research revealed that 68% of design inconsistencies came from permission delays, not skill gaps.

I synthesized these insights into design principles that fundamentally changed our enterprise approach. Instead of building more features, we simplified permission models and created self-service brand centers. This research-driven pivot led to 60% faster adoption rates and several multi-million dollar contract renewals.

Understanding AI-Assisted Creativity Through Longitudinal Studies

As AI becomes a creative partner, understanding how humans and AI collaborate over time becomes critical. I designed and conducted innovative diary studies that followed users' evolving relationships with AI design tools across several weeks.

Rather than one-time usability tests, I created prototypes that participants used in their actual work, documenting their journey from skepticism to integration. My research revealed surprising patterns: users initially over-relied on AI, then pulled back too far, before finding a collaborative sweet spot. I identified key moments where users needed human expertise versus AI efficiency.

These insights led to fundamental design decisions about when to surface AI suggestions versus human controls. The resulting patterns have become the standard for AI-assisted design tools, with engagement metrics showing 73% of users achieving their desired outcomes faster while maintaining creative ownership.
