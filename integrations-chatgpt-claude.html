The Strategic Context

In 2025, the creative industry faced a fundamental shift. The traditional starting point for creative work was rapidly changing as AI assistants like ChatGPT and Claude became the preferred entry point for digital tasks, with ChatGPT usage rising from 1% to 78% as a starting point for searches, while Google searches dropped to just 5%.

‚ö†Ô∏è
The Threat: AI assistants were beginning to offer native design generation capabilities, threatening to bypass traditional design tools entirely. For Canva, this represented both our biggest risk and our greatest opportunity.

The Challenge: Make Canva the default design platform within AI assistants everywhere, achieving feature parity with Canva AI while creating seamless workflow handoffs that preserve user engagement.

Discovery & Strategic Foundation

Understanding the Landscape

My role began with deep strategic analysis of our existing AI integrations. The Canva plugin for ChatGPT was already accounting for 34% of Magic Design usage, but with critical limitations:

Critical Limitations Identified

  • Single-shot generation only - No iteration or conversational flow
  • Limited context awareness - Couldn't access user's existing Canva designs
  • Poor discoverability - Less than 1% of ChatGPT users knew about our integration
  • Fragmented experience - Users had to switch between platforms for editing

Research-Driven Insights

I partnered closely with our research team (led by Marcello Tecleme) to conduct a comprehensive diary study with 10 past CanvaGPT users across Pro, Team, and Enterprise tiers.

üî¨
Research Methodology: Comprehensive diary study with 10 past CanvaGPT users across Pro, Team, and Enterprise tiers to understand critical user needs and pain points.

The research revealed four critical user needs:

Conversational Design Iteration

Users expected back-and-forth refinement like traditional LLMs

Context Integration

AI assistants needed access to users' existing Canva designs and brand assets

Seamless Handoff Points

Clear transition moments between AI assistance and detailed Canva editing

Native Workflow Integration

Design creation should feel embedded in their existing AI workflows

Use Case Prioritization

Through collaborative analysis with Product and Engineering, I identified and prioritized the top use cases based on user research, existing usage data, and technical feasibility:

Tier 1: High Priority

  • Auto-resize social posts into different formats (Stories, Reels, LinkedIn)
  • Brand kit application to ensure consistent visual identity
  • Tone and content adaptation for different audiences and contexts

Tier 2: Medium Priority

  • Dataset autofill for template-based content creation
  • Media upload and design generation for user-provided assets
  • Content search and integration from existing Canva libraries

Key Takeaways

This project fundamentally changed how I think about design in the AI era. Rather than designing isolated features, I learned to design for ecosystem integration and platform thinking.

Key Insights for Future Work

  • Design for Workflows, Not Features: Users don't want another tool‚Äîthey want their existing workflows enhanced.
  • Strategic Design Matters: Sometimes the biggest design decisions happen at the strategy level, not the interface level.
  • Collaboration is Core: Complex platform integration requires deep collaboration across disciplines and organizations.
  • Progressive Enhancement Works: Breaking ambitious visions into achievable phases maintains momentum while building toward transformative outcomes.
