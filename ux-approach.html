üåê I design for ecosystems, not just features

What I keep in mind:

  • Who are all the players, and how do they connect?

I ask a lot of questions (the strategic kind)

My design process isn't about jumping into Figma. It starts with getting aligned: on the problem, the people, and what success even looks like. I've learned that creating space for shared framing before moving to solutions prevents revision cycles and builds stronger stakeholder partnerships.

How I bring structure to ambiguity:

  • Vision workshops and mapping sessions - I facilitate alignment before execution
  • Synthesizing research into design principles - Turning insights into actionable frameworks
  • Communicating trade-offs, not just solutions - Making assumptions and constraints explicit
  • Clarifying ownership and expectations upfront - "Are you looking for me to shape direction here, or execute something you've already got in mind?"

I present work-in-progress with explicit context about assumptions and invite shaping rather than judgment. This approach has strengthened my strategic partnerships and reduced the need for major revisions.

üß† I leverage AI strategically in my workflow

AI is deeply embedded in my day-to-day work, but not as a replacement for design thinking; as an amplifier for productivity and insight generation.

How I use AI to enhance design:

  • Rapid prototyping and iteration - Creating coded prototypes and testing concepts faster
  • Research synthesis at scale - Analyzing large volumes of user feedback to surface patterns

I like sharing the why, not just the what

A polished screen is great, but I care more about showing how I got there.

I try to make my thinking transparent so it's easier to collaborate and build trust.

How I do this:

  • I annotate flows, not just present them - Context and rationale travel with the work
  • I use 1-pagers to bring folks into the process - Structured thinking documents that align teams
  • I write to reflect, not just to document - Capturing assumptions and learnings for future work
  • I present work-in-progress with explicit context - Inviting the right kind of feedback at the right time

I'm a big believer that clarity and storytelling are core design skills, not just nice add-ons. They enable better collaboration and stronger strategic influence.

I believe in good vibes and clear comms

UX is collaborative. I work closely with triads, engineers, and other designers, and I'm big on giving credit, asking thoughtful questions, and keeping momentum without burning people out.

What collaboration looks like for me:

  • Clarity > cleverness - Simple, direct communication that moves work forward
  • Feedback early and often - Creating space for input before decisions are locked
  • Helping unblock others when I can - Proactive problem-solving and knowledge sharing
  • Keeping conversations human, even in complex spaces - Building relationships alongside delivering work
  • Coaching design outcomes - Helping team members grow their craft and confidence
  • Championing team wins - Recognizing contributions and fostering cross-functional energy

I've found that investing in team relationships and communication patterns pays dividends in project outcomes and long-term collaboration quality.
